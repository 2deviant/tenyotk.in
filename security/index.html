Canons of Security

Information always has been and always will be the most powerful weapon. In the hands of skilled manipulators, that information can be quite damanging. In the hands of amateurs, it can be devastating. Information is power, hide it well.

Canons of Security

If your secrets are worth a lot of $: do everything yourself

Society is based on reciprocity (a.k.a. stick and carrot): if you do something "bad", then "bad" things will happen to you. If the likelihood of "bad" consequences is decreased or altogether eliminated, the reciprocity barrier disappears and one is free to do anything one desires. To that end, any organization or an individual with an iota of security conscience shall not permit unauthorized personnel — such as custodians — to enter the premises where private/confidential/secret information is held/handled/discussed; whether those individuals are escorted or not. Under most ideal and humane circumstances, bribing a member of the service staff to install a surveillance device on your premises, relocating them permanently to small town in South America never to be seen again (to escape prosecution for their crime)... costs around $2M. If the perpetrators are not as concerned about the well-being of their informant(s), it costs far, far less. Keep that in mind when constructing safeguards around your secrets.

The machine must be harder to corrupt than a human

The goal of electronic security (counter)measures is to make electronic penetration prohibitively more expensive — in either time or money or both — than the physical compromise of the system. That is, if it is cheaper/easier/faster to hire/bribe an employee/ninja/custodian to plant a surveillance device in one’s office, one’s car, one’s computer keyboard, etc. then the electronic security is a success.
